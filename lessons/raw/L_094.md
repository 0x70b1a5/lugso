Participles are like adjectives. A participle derives from a verb to create a description of a noun. 

### verb-byoÌ£: "the ... that verbed" 

This participle indicates that the subject actively peformed the action.

`dig earth-PROL deity_bestial speak-PTCP.PAST.ACT`

_The animal-god, who spoke, digs through earth._

### verb-dyoÌ£: "the ... that was verbed"

This participle indicates that some action was performed to the subject.

### verb-zyoÌ£: "the ... that has not verbed" or "the unverbed ..."

This participle indicates that some action has not been performed by the subject. As a participle that English lacks, it may take some getting used to. It is approximately equal in meaning to **dyoÌ£ziÌ£** `PTCP.PAST.PASS-NEG`, and that is how it is usually translated.
