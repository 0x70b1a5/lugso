## Possessive suffix

In English, when something belongs to someone, it is _someone's_ something. We add _'s_ to the possessing noun.

In Lugso, similarly, the cultist adds **-guÌ£**.

## Possessive vs. Genitive

Speakers of Latin will be confused at the inclusion of both the possessive suffix and the genitive case in Lugso. "What is the difference between "my blood" _the blood of my veins_, and "my blood" _the blood that I own (in that jar over there)_ ?"

In Lugso, Genitive is used to indicate origin and relation - hence **lugÌ£soÌ£** _of blood_. Possessive, by contrast, is strictly property/dominion/possession - **lugÌ£soÌ£ gu5Ì£liÌ£Ì£dÌ£soÌ£ sotÌ£dÌ£soÌ£** _of blood of gods of thoughts_ - not "thoughts' gods' blood's". Hence, the child that a mother gives birth to is both **yolsÌ£ rihÌ£soÌ£** and **yolsÌ£ rihÌ£guÌ£** - his mother's child in origin, and (until he is an adult) his mother's child in dominion.
